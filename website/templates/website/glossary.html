Glossary

The domain of digitized curriculum standards comprises several interacting components that support the provision of published standards and use of OER. Learn more in the report on digitizing curriculum standards.


Curriculum standards: the set of standards, objectives, and concepts required to be taught and learned in a given subject, within each grade level, and subdivided into topics, subtopics, and individual standard statements. Curriculum standards are accessible  as print books or PDF documents published by a government organization that has the authority to define curriculum standards within the local jurisdiction.

Digitization: the conversion of a curriculum standards document into a structured data format like  spreadsheets or a database.

Publishing: making curriculum standard data publicly available for access, browsing, download, reference, and, in some cases, approval by a curricular body. 

Standard statement: an individual entry within a digitized standard, which could be folder-like (intermediate levels of the hierarchy) or an atomic statement. Each statement describes a particular set of competencies expected of learners at that level.

Official identifiers: short human-readable tags that uniquely identify particular standard statements within a document. For example, the code 2.MD.D.10 within the U.S. Common Core Math standards, or the code B4.4.1.2 within the Ghana Math standards.

Digitized standards: the digital representation of a curriculum standard document is modeled as a hierarchical collection (tree structure) of standards statements. Digitized standards allow curriculum documents from different countries to be represented using a common data model that enables data interoperability between different systems.

Content Correlations: a mapping between standard statements and learning resources suitable for teaching or assessing these standards. Content correlations are made of individual associations between learning resources and relevant standard statements.

Standards crosswalk: a mapping between statements within different standards. Standards crosswalks are made of individual alignment relations that identify statements from one standard to be equivalent or similar to statements in another standard.
